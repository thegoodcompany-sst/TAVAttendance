TAVA Attendance

A live attendance system for a tuition centre

Students tap their own name on an iPad to sign in. Staff see it live on the web. It keeps working when the Wi-Fi doesn't.

The problem

TAVA is a Singapore tuition centre — Maths on Mondays, English and Reading on Thursdays, plus a drop-in study space through the week. Attendance was a paper register: slow to take, easy to lose, and impossible to answer simple questions from ("how often has this student been late this term?").

TAVA Attendance replaces the paper register with three connected pieces:

  • An iPad kiosk at the door where students sign themselves in.
  • A web dashboard where staff watch attendance live and pull reports.
  • A shared backend that keeps everything in sync and works offline.

What it does

  • Self sign-in. Students tap their name. No teacher standing at the door with a clipboard.
  • Automatic late tracking. The kiosk compares the sign-in time against the class start time and marks late on its own.
  • Offline-safe. If the network drops, sign-ins are stored on the device and synced automatically when it returns — no lost records, no duplicates.
  • Parent visibility. A read-only view for parents of their own child (staged behind a feature flag).

The walkthrough

The same beats shown in the live demo.

1. Student signs in

A student taps their card on the kiosk. It turns green if they're on time, or orange if class has already started — decided automatically from the class schedule.

2. Staff overrides

Staff can correct any card on the spot: mark Late, mark Not Here (which lets the student tap in again), or mark Absent. When a parent collects a child early, the card goes purple — Dismissed — the student still counts as present, just signed out early.

3. Offline, then back online

With Wi-Fi off, a sign-in still works and shows a small pending indicator. When the connection returns, it syncs quietly in the background and the indicator clears.

4. Live dashboard

The web dashboard shows today's sessions and attendance, refreshing every 30 seconds — a sign-in on the iPad appears on screen moments later.

5. Analytics & student history

Attendance and punctuality percentages per student per class, and a per-student history of recent sessions. The paperwork a parent-teacher meeting used to need, ready in a click.

6. Parent portal

Parents see a read-only view scoped to their own child only.

Where it stands

Live in production

The kiosk, the live dashboard, analytics, student history, offline sync, and staff overrides are running in production today.

Staged behind flags

The parent portal and push notifications are built but shipped dark behind feature flags — turned on the moment they're ready, with no redeploy.

What's next

  • Parent notifications — alert a parent automatically when their child is marked late or absent.
  • Android app — a second kiosk platform alongside iOS.
  • PDPA compliance work — completing the data-protection groundwork for handling children's personal data.
